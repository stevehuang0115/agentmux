# Insurance Industry Context

## Common Terminology
- **Premium**: The amount the policyholder pays for the insurance coverage.
- **Deductible**: The amount the policyholder must pay out-of-pocket before the insurance company pays a claim.
- **Claim**: A formal request by a policyholder to an insurance company for coverage or compensation for a covered loss or policy event.
- **Policy**: A contract between the insurer and the insured, which determines the claims which the insurer is legally required to pay.
- **Endorsement**: An amendment or addition to an existing insurance contract which changes the terms or scope of the original policy.
- **Underwriting**: The process through which an individual or institution takes on financial risk for a fee.
- **Actuary**: A business professional who deals with the measurement and management of risk and uncertainty.

## Core Processes
1. **Underwriting & Quoting**: Evaluating a new applicant's risk and providing a price for coverage.
2. **Policy Issuance**: Formally creating and delivering the policy document.
3. **Billing & Collections**: Managing premium payments.
4. **Claims Adjudication**: The process of determining whether to pay, deny, or negotiate a claim.
5. **Renewal Management**: Evaluating policies near their expiration and offering continued coverage.

## Insurance Lines for SMBs
- **General Liability (GL)**: Covers legal costs if a business is sued for injury or property damage.
- **Professional Liability (E&O)**: Protects against claims of negligence or mistakes in professional services.
- **Workers' Compensation**: Provides benefits to employees who are injured on the job.
- **Business Owners Policy (BOP)**: Combines liability and property insurance into a single package for small businesses.
